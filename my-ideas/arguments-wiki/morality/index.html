Absolutism

Moral absolutism holds that there are actions (potentially all) that are intrinsically moral or immoral, regardless of subjective opinions, local customs, context or consequences.

Counterarguments

  • Absolutist morality has no merit due to it's definitional independence from rationality and consequences. This makes it completely arbitrary.
  • Any conception of "absolute morality" is only absolute according to the person who holds it, making it externally subjective.
  • It sounds like a very dogmatic pure simulacrum.
  • Absolutely all actions being intrinsically moral or immoral entails that there are binary moral values for activating certain neurons, displacing your ear lobes, digesting food or committing quantum collapse in the molecule at the tip of your median-length eyelash. Something about ontology or prime/composite actions or whatever. I'll get more into this later but think about how absurd it is

Subjectivism

Moral subjectivism is the view that morality is a subjective phenomenon based on the beliefs of individuals.

  • The adoption of any particular moral framework is subjective. Regardless of the implications of adhering to any particular framework, everyone's perception of all frameworks is based on perceiving, processing and interpreting an arbitrary set of information - in other words, the subject becomes subject to the subject. The concept of any moral system that isn't subjective to at least some degree is utterly superficial.

Consequentialism

  • The adoption of any particular moral framework is ultimately consequential. Regardless of anyone's specific reasons for doing so, the ultimate reason for someone following a certain form of morality is that their brains were trained, programmed, manipulated or otherwise convinced to do so, and so they do it. The concept of any moral system that isn't based on consequentialism is utterly superficial
    • I'll sort this out later but also - adopting any moral framework means that you've focused your consequentialism on whether you're following the framework or not.
